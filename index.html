    1. Three main differences between web1.0, web2.0 and web3.0:
      1. Web1.0 is considered a "read-only static web" web, while web 2.0 is considered a "read-write interactive web" web and web3.0 as a "read-write intelligent web".
      2. On web1.0, companies publish content and the rest of the users are consumers. On web2.0, companies build platforms to let people publish their content and interact with others. On web3.0, applications are built for interactions, companies build platforms for knowledge sharing and to let people publish services.
      3. Web1.0 is company-oriented (companies post their information and customers consume that data), web2.0 is community-oriented - platforms such as Facebook were developed for enhancing web-socializing. Web3.0 is individual-oriented - each person communicates with his applications, knowledge is accessible to the individual.
    2. Usecase example - medications side effects
      1. On web1.0 - I would have to go to the pharmaceutical company's website and look for the side-effects description of the desired medication. I could use a search engine and try to type my query, but many times results are inaccurate.
      2. On web2.0 - I would use a search engine and would type the query "side effect Optalgin". Google, for example, initiated the PageRank algorithm. Therefore, I expect to find a relevant result quickly.
      3. On web3.0 - I can simply type the query "Optalgin" and find it's side effects either in the information box made by Google, or one of the top searching results.
